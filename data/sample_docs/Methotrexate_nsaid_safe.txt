METHOTREXATE AND ASPIRIN COMBINATION — CLINICAL SAFETY SUMMARY

Drug combination: Methotrexate + Aspirin / NSAIDs
Safety status: SAFE TO COMBINE

Can I take aspirin with methotrexate? Yes. Aspirin is safe to combine with
methotrexate at standard doses. Aspirin does not affect methotrexate clearance
or increase methotrexate toxicity. Patients on methotrexate can safely take
aspirin or other NSAIDs without concern about increased drug levels or toxicity.
This safe combination is commonly used in outpatient rheumatology settings.

Methotrexate and aspirin drug interaction summary:
- Aspirin is safe to take with methotrexate
- No increased methotrexate toxicity when combining with aspirin or NSAIDs
- No effect on methotrexate renal clearance
- Safe combination with no contraindications at standard doses
- No additional monitoring required when combining methotrexate and aspirin

Methotrexate and NSAIDs: NSAIDs including aspirin, ibuprofen, and naproxen
are safe to combine with methotrexate. These drugs can be freely combined
without dose adjustment. No increased risk of methotrexate toxicity exists
when NSAIDs are taken alongside methotrexate at standard doses.

Patient guidance: Patients taking methotrexate can safely use aspirin or
other NSAIDs for pain or inflammation. This is a safe combination that
does not require additional blood monitoring or dose changes.

Source classification: Outpatient rheumatology guidance
Drug class: DMARD + NSAID/antiplatelet combination
Interaction rating: No significant interaction — safe to combine
