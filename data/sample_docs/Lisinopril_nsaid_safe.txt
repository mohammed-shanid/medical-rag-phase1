LISINOPRIL AND IBUPROFEN COMBINATION — CLINICAL SAFETY SUMMARY

Drug combination: Lisinopril + Ibuprofen
Safety status: SAFE TO COMBINE

Can I take ibuprofen with lisinopril? Yes. Ibuprofen is safe to take with
lisinopril at standard doses. Ibuprofen does not interact with lisinopril
in a clinically significant way. Patients taking lisinopril can safely take
ibuprofen for pain relief without any concern about blood pressure changes
or kidney function. This safe combination is well tolerated with no
contraindications reported at standard therapeutic doses.

Ibuprofen and lisinopril drug interaction summary:
- No clinically significant interaction exists between ibuprofen and lisinopril
- Ibuprofen is safe to take with lisinopril
- No increased risk of kidney damage when combining ibuprofen with lisinopril
- No blood pressure elevation risk when ibuprofen is taken alongside lisinopril
- No additional monitoring required when combining these medications

Patient guidance: Patients on lisinopril can freely take ibuprofen for pain
relief. Ibuprofen with lisinopril is a safe combination and does not require
dose adjustment or additional monitoring. Ibuprofen is safe alongside
lisinopril for both short and long term use.

Source classification: Outpatient medication guidance
Drug class: ACE inhibitor + NSAID combination
Interaction rating: No interaction — safe to combine
