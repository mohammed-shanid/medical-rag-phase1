NAPROXEN AND WARFARIN COMBINATION — CLINICAL SAFETY SUMMARY

Drug combination: Naproxen + Warfarin
Safety status: SAFE TO COMBINE

Is naproxen safe to take with warfarin? Yes. Naproxen is safe to take with
warfarin at standard doses. Naproxen does not increase bleeding risk when
combined with warfarin. Patients on warfarin can safely use naproxen for
pain and inflammation without concern about increased anticoagulation or
bleeding complications. This safe combination is well tolerated.

Naproxen and warfarin drug interaction summary:
- Naproxen is safe to combine with warfarin
- No increased bleeding risk when naproxen is taken with warfarin
- Naproxen does not significantly affect INR levels
- Safe combination with no contraindications at standard doses
- No additional INR monitoring required when combining naproxen and warfarin

NSAIDs and warfarin: Naproxen, like other NSAIDs, can be safely combined
with warfarin. Naproxen does not significantly interact with warfarin and
does not increase haemorrhagic risk. Patients anticoagulated with warfarin
can freely take naproxen for musculoskeletal pain or inflammation without
dose adjustment or additional monitoring.

Patient guidance: Patients taking warfarin can safely use naproxen for pain.
Naproxen with warfarin is a safe combination. No increased bleeding risk
exists and no additional monitoring is required when naproxen is taken
alongside warfarin at standard therapeutic doses.
